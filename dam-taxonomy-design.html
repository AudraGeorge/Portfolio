DAM PORTFOLIO PIECE 3: TAXONOMY DESIGN

Creative Asset Library Taxonomy Design

Deloitte Global Creative Operations Implementation Period: 2018‚Äì2022

Overview

This taxonomy was designed to organize photography, templates, brand assets, marketing materials, and video content for Deloitte's global creative operations team. The structure supports a 40+ member team across multiple regions, enabling efficient asset discovery, brand consistency, and seamless collaboration across time zones.

The taxonomy organizes 25,000+ creative assets spanning corporate photography, presentation templates, brand guidelines, marketing collateral, and video content.

Primary Goals:
  • Enable fast, intuitive asset retrieval for designers, marketers, and creative teams
  • Support global collaboration (assets accessible across regions and time zones)
  • Accommodate growth without requiring structural overhaul
  • Maintain clear separation between asset types while enabling cross-category search

    • Design Principles

    1. Asset-Type-First Organization

    Content organized by type of asset (photography, template, logo, video) rather than by project, campaign, or date.

    Why: Creatives search by what they need ("I need a photo of an office" or "I need a client pitch template"), not by when it was created or which campaign it belonged to. Asset-type-first navigation aligns with user mental models. Example: 
    ‚úÖ Good: Photography > Corporate_Lifestyle > Office_Environments > [Photos]

    ‚ùå Bad: 2023 > Q2_Campaign > Photos > Office_Environments

    2. Shallow Hierarchy (Maximum 3 Levels)

    Folder depth limited to prevent "click fatigue" and ensure assets remain discoverable with minimal navigation.

    Example: 
    ‚úÖ Good: Templates > Presentation_Templates > Client_Decks > [Files]

    ‚ùå Bad: Resources > Creative > Templates > PPT > Client > Pitch_Decks > Standard > [Files]

    3. Campaign/Project Nesting Within Asset Type

    Campaign-specific assets nested within their asset type category, not the reverse.

    Why: Campaigns end, but asset types remain constant. Organizing by campaign at the top level creates structural churn as campaigns launch and conclude. Example: 
    ‚úÖ Good: Marketing_Materials > Campaign_Assets > Q2_Launch > [Campaign assets]

    ‚ùå Bad: Q2_Launch_Campaign > Photos, Templates, Videos, Collateral

    4. Clear Active vs. Archive Separation

    Archived assets physically separated from active library to keep working directories clean and reduce cognitive load during search.

    5. Regional Variations Integrated, Not Isolated

    Regional asset variations nested within functional categories rather than creating separate regional folders at top level.

    Why: Maintains browsability across regions while accommodating localization needs. Example: 
    ‚úÖ Good: Logos > Regional_Variations > APAC_Logos, EMEA_Logos

    ‚ùå Bad: [Top Level] APAC_Assets, EMEA_Assets, Americas_Assets (each with full structure duplicated)

    • File Naming Conventions

    Standard Format

    [AssetType]_[Category]_[Description]_[Region]_[Version]_[Date].extension

    Asset Type Prefixes

  • Photo: Photography (corporate, lifestyle, industry, stock)
  • Template: Presentation, design, or document template
  • Logo: Logos and brand marks
  • BrandGuide: Brand guidelines and style documentation
  • ColorPalette: Color palette documentation
  • Typography: Typography guidelines and font documentation
  • Campaign: Campaign-specific marketing assets
  • Collateral: Evergreen marketing collateral
  • Event: Event-specific materials
  • Video: Video content (brand, case study, thought leadership, stock)

    Scalability Considerations

    Adding New Asset Types

  • Assess whether new type fits within existing Level 1 categories
  • Example: "Podcast Audio" could nest under existing structure as 05_Video_Assets > Audio_Podcasts or create new 06_Audio_Assets if volume justifies

    • Adding New Campaigns

  • Create new campaign folder under 04_Marketing_Materials > Campaign_Assets > [Campaign_Name]
  • Maintain standard subfolder structure (Print_Ads, Digital_Banners, Email_Graphics, Social_Media)
  • Archive campaign folder when concluded

    • Adding New Regions

  • Nest regional variations within asset type folders (not separate top-level regional folders)
  • Example: Logos > Regional_Variations > LatAm_Logos

    • Adding New Industries

  • Create new industry subfolder under existing categories
  • Example: Photography > Industry_Specific > Energy_Sector

    • Team Growth

  • Taxonomy designed for asset type, not individuals or teams
  • Reorganizations don't require restructuring asset library
