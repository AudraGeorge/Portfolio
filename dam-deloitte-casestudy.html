DAM PORTFOLIO PIECE 1: DELOITTE CASE STUDY

Creative Asset Organization & Workflow Optimization for Global Team

Client: Deloitte (Global Consulting Firm) Role: Creative Operations Specialist - Global Team Timeline: 2018 â€“ August 2022 Tools: Adobe Experience Manager (AEM), SharePoint, Adobe Workfront, Adobe Creative Suite

PROJECT OVERVIEW

Background

Deloitte's global creative team produces marketing collateral, presentations, brand materials, campaign assets, and client deliverables across multiple regions. As the team expanded from U.S./Canada operations to serve international markets (EMEA, APAC, Latin America), the volume of creative assets grew exponentially - from managing approximately 5,000 assets to over 25,000+ files spanning photography, templates, brand guidelines, presentation decks, and marketing materials.

Challenge

Rapid global expansion created a fragmented creative asset ecosystem with no centralized organization, inconsistent naming conventions, and duplicated files scattered across regional drives. Creative assets critical to maintaining brand consistency and supporting global campaigns were difficult to locate, version control was chaotic, and teams in different time zones couldn't efficiently access or share resources.

Key Pain Points:
  • Creative assets scattered across 12+ regional SharePoint sites and local drives with no central repository
  • No standardized folder structure or taxonomy for organizing visual assets, templates, and brand materials
  • Inconsistent file naming made search nearly impossible (6+ naming patterns in use)
  • 30% estimated duplicate files (same asset uploaded to multiple locations by different regions)
  • Poor metadata tagging meant AEM search functionality was underutilized
  • Version control chaos: Multiple versions of same template/asset with no clear indication of which was current
  • Cross-regional asset sharing challenges: APAC team couldn't find assets created by EMEA team and vice versa

    • Objective

    Consolidate, organize, and optimize creative asset management across the global team by:

  • Centralizing all creative assets in Adobe Experience Manager (AEM)
  • Implementing a scalable taxonomy for organizing visual assets, templates, and brand materials
  • Establishing metadata standards to enable efficient search and retrieval
  • Creating file naming conventions for consistency and version control
  • Eliminating duplicate files and optimizing storage
  • Enabling seamless cross-regional asset sharing and collaboration

    • ASSESSMENT & DISCOVERY

    Discovery Process

    Phase 1: Asset Inventory (Weeks 1-4)

    Conducted comprehensive audit of existing creative assets across all platforms:

    Asset Census:
  • Cataloged 25,000+ files across 12 regional SharePoint sites
  • Analyzed file types: Photography (32%), InDesign templates (24%), PowerPoint templates (18%), Brand guidelines/PDFs (15%), Video assets (8%), Other (3%)
  • Mapped current folder structures and identified organizational patterns (or lack thereof)
    • User Interviews (18 creatives and stakeholders):
  • How do you currently find assets you need?
  • What types of assets do you use most frequently?
  • What pain points do you experience with current system?
  • What would make asset retrieval faster/easier?
    • Workflow Observation:
  • Shadowed team members searching for assets (average search time: 12-15 minutes per asset)
  • Documented common search strategies and failure points
  • Identified high-value asset types requiring fastest access
    • Phase 2: Gap Analysis (Week 5) Critical Findings: 1. No Central Asset Repository
  • Finding: Creative assets spread across 12+ locations with no single source of truth
  • Asset Locations:

    • - Regional SharePoint sites (U.S., Canada, UK, Germany, France, Australia, Singapore, each with own structure)

    - Individual team member's local drives

    - Email attachments (assets shared via email, not uploaded to shared location)

    - Adobe Creative Cloud Libraries (partial, inconsistent usage)

  • Impact: Average 12-15 minutes to locate a single asset; frequent "can someone send me the [asset]?" requests
    • 2. Folder Structure Chaos
  • Finding: Each region developed its own organizational system with no alignment
  • Examples:

    • - U.S. organized by campaign name

    - EMEA organized by date

    - APAC organized by asset type

    - No consistent approach to organizing templates, photography, or brand materials

  • Impact: Even when assets were in AEM, inconsistent structure made navigation difficult
    • 3. Naming Convention Nightmare
  • Finding: 6 different naming patterns in use simultaneously
  • Examples:

    • - IMG_5432.jpg (camera default)

    - Deloitte_Hero_Image_Final.jpg (no date, no version)

    - Hero-Image-v3-FINAL-FINAL.indd (multiple "final" versions)

    - 2023_Q2_Campaign_Hero.jpg (date-first format)

    - DTC_Hero_032023_v1.jpg (abbreviated, cryptic)

  • Impact: Search by filename rarely successful; heavy reliance on visual browsing
    • 4. Metadata Gaps
  • Finding: 68% of assets in AEM had incomplete or missing metadata
  • Missing Metadata Breakdown:

    • - Asset type/category: 71% missing

    - Campaign/project: 65% missing

    - Region/market: 58% missing

    - Usage rights: 82% missing

    - Keywords/tags: 77% missing

  • Impact: AEM's powerful search functionality rendered almost useless
    • 5. Duplicate File Epidemic
  • Finding: Estimated 30% of assets were duplicates (7,500+ duplicate files)
  • Root Causes:

    • - Regional teams uploaded same asset to their local SharePoint for "easier access"

    - Same asset renamed and uploaded as "new" file

    - Lack of version control led to saving multiple near-identical variants

    - No central repository meant teams didn't know asset already existed

  • Impact: Storage costs, confusion over which version to use, wasted time uploading duplicates
    • 6. Template Version Control Crisis
  • Finding: PowerPoint and InDesign templates had 8-12 versions in circulation, no clear "current" version
  • Consequences:

    • - Teams using outdated brand templates

    - Inconsistent brand presentation across regions

    - Rework required when wrong template version used

  • Impact: Brand inconsistency; wasted time redoing work with correct template

    • SOLUTION DESIGN

    Centralized Asset Repository in Adobe Experience Manager

    Migrated all creative assets to AEM as single source of truth, organized with a scalable, function-based taxonomy.

    Taxonomy Design Principles:
  • Asset-type-first organization: Creatives search by type of asset needed (photo, template, logo), not by project or date
  • Numbered prefixes: Enforce logical navigation order
  • Campaign/project nesting: Campaigns nested within asset type (not vice versa) for flexibility
  • Regional variations integrated: Regional assets nested within functional categories (not separate regional folders)
  • Clear archive separation: Archive at end (99 prefix) to keep active assets clean

    Naming Rules:
  • No spaces (use underscores)
  • Asset type prefix (Photo, Template, Logo, Campaign, etc.)
  • Region tag: Global, EMEA, APAC, Americas, or specific country
  • Version format: v## (v01, v02, v03) - leading zero for proper sorting
  • Date format: YYYYMMDD (20230415)
  • Maximum length: 100 characters
  • Descriptive but concise: Readable filenames that describe content

    • Metadata Schema

    Defined comprehensive metadata requirements to enable powerful search and filtering:

    FieldTypeRequiredPurposeExamples
    Asset TypeDropdownYesPrimary classificationPhotography, Template, Logo, Brand Asset, Video
    CategoryDropdownYesSecondary classificationLifestyle, Portrait, Corporate, Product
    DescriptionTextYesShort description"Team collaboration in modern office"
    Region/MarketMulti-selectYesGeographic applicabilityGlobal, EMEA, APAC, Americas, [Country]
    Campaign/ProjectDropdownNoAssociated initiativeQ2 Launch, Annual Report 2023, [Client Name]
    IndustryMulti-selectNoIndustry focusFinancial Services, Technology, Healthcare, Government

    OrientationDropdownNoLayout orientationHorizontal, Vertical, Square
    Color PaletteTagsNoDominant colorsBlue, Green, Neutral, Warm Tones
    Metadata Enforcement:
  • Required fields must be completed before asset upload (AEM workflow enforcement)
  • Dropdown menus prevent inconsistent free-text entries
  • Multi-select fields enable faceted search (filter by region + industry + asset type)
  • Metadata templates for bulk uploads

    • Version Control System

    Implemented clear versioning standards for templates and frequently updated assets:

    Version Naming:
  • v01: Initial version
  • v02, v03, etc.: Minor updates (content changes, small design tweaks)
  • v10, v20, etc.: Major updates (significant redesign, rebrand)
    • "Current" Version Management:
  • File Status metadata field: Mark current version as "Current," older versions as "Archive"
  • Collection feature in AEM: "Current Templates" collection auto-populates with latest versions
  • Archive old versions but retain for reference (moved to 99_Archive folder)
    • Version History Documentation:
  • Version notes captured in AEM metadata: "Version 03: Updated logo per 2023 rebrand"
  • Major version changes communicated to team via Slack announcement

    • Governance & Maintenance Standards

    Created asset management governance to sustain organization over time:

    Upload Standards:
  • All new assets must follow folder structure, naming conventions, and metadata requirements
  • Duplicate check required before uploading (search AEM to verify asset doesn't already exist)
  • Templates must be marked with version number and reviewed for brand compliance
    • Approval Workflow:
  • New brand assets and templates routed through brand team for approval before publishing
  • Campaign assets reviewed by creative lead
  • Stock photography validated for licensing compliance
    • Quarterly Audits:
  • Review metadata completeness across random sample (500 assets)
  • Identify and consolidate duplicate files
  • Archive assets unused for 18+ months (photography) or 12+ months (campaign materials)
  • Update "Current Templates" collection
    • Regional Coordinators:
  • Designated one "Asset Champion" per region to:

    • - Ensure regional teams follow standards

    - Answer questions about asset organization

    - Flag regional-specific needs or issues

    IMPLEMENTATION & OUTCOMES

    Implementation Approach

    Phase 1: AEM Setup & Taxonomy Build (Weeks 6-8)
  • Configured AEM folder structure
  • Created metadata schemas and dropdown values
  • Set up upload workflows with required field validation
  • Built "Current Templates" collection
    • Phase 2: Pilot Migration (Weeks 9-10)
  • Migrated U.S. photography and templates as proof of concept (2,500 assets)
  • Tested naming conventions and metadata tagging
  • Solicited feedback from U.S. creative team
  • Refined structure based on usability testing
    • Phase 3: Bulk Asset Migration (Weeks 11-18)
  • Systematically migrated remaining 22,500+ assets from regional SharePoint sites
  • Bulk metadata tagging where possible (asset type, region)
  • Manual review of high-value assets for accuracy
  • Renamed files to meet naming conventions
    • Phase 4: Duplicate Removal (Weeks 19-20)
  • Identified duplicate assets using hash comparison and visual review
  • Consolidated duplicates: kept highest-resolution version, archived or deleted lower-quality copies
  • Freed approximately 40GB of storage
    • Phase 5: Training & Rollout (Weeks 21-22)
  • Conducted live training sessions across all time zones (5 sessions for global coverage)
  • Distributed quick reference guides for naming conventions and metadata
  • Created video tutorials: "How to Search AEM," "How to Upload Assets Correctly"
  • Designated regional Asset Champions
    • Phase 6: Monitoring & Refinement (Weeks 23-26)
  • Weekly check-ins with regional teams to address questions
  • Minor metadata dropdown adjustments based on user feedback
  • Established quarterly audit schedule

    • Outcomes & Impact

    Efficiency Gains:
  • Asset retrieval time reduced by 65%: Average search time dropped from 12-15 minutes to 4-5 minutes
  • Duplicate files eliminated: Removed 7,500+ duplicate assets, freeing 40GB storage
  • Metadata completeness increased to 96%: Nearly all assets fully searchable and filterable
  • Template version clarity: "Current Templates" collection eliminated confusion over which version to use
    • Operational Improvements:
  • Centralized repository: Single source of truth eliminated cross-regional asset sharing challenges
  • Version control: Clear versioning system reduced rework from using outdated templates
  • Brand consistency: Current templates easily accessible ensured global brand alignment
  • Search functionality: Metadata-driven search enabled finding assets by region, industry, campaign, or keyword
    • Cross-Regional Collaboration:
  • APAC team could access EMEA photography without asking colleagues or re-creating assets
  • Templates standardized globally with regional variations clearly tagged
  • Reduced duplicate creation: Before uploading new asset, teams searched AEM first
    • User Satisfaction:
  • Post-implementation survey: 94% of creative team rated new AEM system as "significantly better" or "much easier to use"
  • Reduced Slack messages requesting asset locations by an estimated 75%
  • Regional Asset Champions reported smooth adoption with minimal ongoing support needed
    • Scalability:
  • Taxonomy easily accommodated new asset types (added social media templates, video assets in 2021)
  • New regions (LatAm team added in 2021) integrated seamlessly into existing structure
  • System remained organized 4+ years post-launch with minimal intervention

    • Governance Framework Excerpt

    Creative Asset Upload Checklist:

    Before uploading any asset to Adobe Experience Manager:

  • [ ] Asset is final/approved version (not draft or work-in-progress)
  • [ ] File named correctly: [AssetType]_[Category]_[Description]_[Region]_[Version]_[Date].extension
  • [ ] Searched AEM to confirm asset doesn't already exist (no duplicates)
  • [ ] Saved in correct folder based on asset type and category
  • [ ] All required metadata completed:

    • - [ ] Asset Type (Photography, Template, Logo, etc.)

    - [ ] Region/Market

    - [ ] Usage Rights

    - [ ] File Status (Current or Archive)

    - [ ] Keywords (minimum 3 relevant tags)

  • [ ] If template: Version number updated and marked as "Current" in File Status
  • [ ] High-resolution file (not low-res or screen-resolution only)
    • If unsure about any step, contact your regional Asset Champion.

    CONCLUSION

    Consolidating and organizing Deloitte's global creative assets in Adobe Experience Manager transformed a fragmented, inefficient system into a centralized, searchable repository that enabled seamless collaboration across regions. By implementing a scalable taxonomy, enforcing metadata standards, establishing clear naming conventions, and creating governance frameworks, the creative team reduced asset retrieval time by 65%, eliminated thousands of duplicate files, and ensured brand consistency globally.

    Key Success Factors:
  • User-centered taxonomy design: Structure aligned with how creatives actually search for assets (by type, not by project or date)
  • Comprehensive metadata schema: Enabled powerful filtering and search capabilities
  • Clear governance and training: Sustained organization over time and across regional teams
  • Regional Asset Champions: Ensured adoption and compliance without creating bottlenecks

The centralized AEM asset library continues to support Deloitte's global creative operations, scaling seamlessly as the team and asset volume grow.
