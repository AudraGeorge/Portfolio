About Me

I've built information systems at Deloitte and Orvis, managing everything from global digital asset libraries to enterprise-wide knowledge bases. My work focuses on the infrastructure no one thinks about until it breaks: taxonomy, metadata, workflow documentation, and governance standards.

My background combines hands-on corporate DAM experience with formal training in information organization (M.A. Historic Preservation—essentially library science applied to objects and history). I specialize in taking systems that have collapsed under their own weight and rebuilding them to be findable, usable, and sustainable.

My Approach

Information systems fail when they lack structure, standards, or documentation. My work is building the missing infrastructure.

Taxonomy & Metadata

The fix isn't better folders—it's metadata. I build controlled vocabularies and classification systems that make assets searchable and filterable instead of guessable.

Information Architecture

When the structure makes no sense, I rebuild it. Clear hierarchies, logical groupings, and consistent naming conventions—so people can find things without needing a map.

Workflow & Process

Vague email requests and hallway conversations don't scale. I design intake forms, project hubs, and standardized handoff processes that turn chaos into trackable work.

Governance & Standards

Systems collapse without documentation. I write the SOPs, style guides, and standards that keep things consistent—especially when team members change or scale increases.

Search & Discoverability

This is the proof it works. When taxonomy, structure, and metadata are done right, people can actually find what they need. Faster searches, fewer duplicates, less time wasted.
