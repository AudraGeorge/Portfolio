Creative Operations Knowledge Base Taxonomy Design

Deloitte Global Creative Operations Implementation Period: 2018â€“2022

Overview

This taxonomy was designed to organize documentation, templates, brand resources, and training materials for a global creative operations team scaling from U.S./Canada to international operations. The structure supports a 40+ member team across multiple time zones, enabling asynchronous collaboration and self-service access to critical operational knowledge.

Primary Goals:
  • Enable fast, intuitive information retrieval across global team
  • Support asynchronous work (reduce dependency on asking colleagues)
  • Accommodate growth without requiring structural overhaul
  • Maintain clear separation between operational docs, brand resources, and training materials

    • Design Principles

    1. Function-Based Organization

    Content organized by what users need to accomplish (intake a project, request an asset, complete legal review) rather than by team, role, or individual.

    Why: Teams reorganize; roles evolve. Organizing by function creates a stable structure that survives organizational changes.

    2. Shallow Hierarchy (Maximum 3 Levels)

    Folder depth limited to prevent "click fatigue" and ensure content remains discoverable.

    Example: 
    âœ… Good: SOPs > Legal & Compliance > Brand Review Process

    âŒ Bad: Resources > Operations > Documentation > SOPs > Compliance > Legal > Brand > Review Process

    3. Parallel Structure Across Categories

    Similar subcategories used across major sections to create predictable navigation patterns.

    Example: Both "SOPs" and "Training Materials" contain subfolders for:
  • Intake & Project Management
  • Asset Management & DAM
  • Legal & Brand Compliance

    • 4. Clear Active vs. Archive Separation

    Archived content physically separated from active resources to keep working directories clean and reduce cognitive load.

    5. Scalability by Design

    Structure accommodates new regions, tools, and workflows without requiring reorganization. Regional variations nested within functional categories rather than creating separate regional folders.

    Metadata Schema

    For assets stored in Adobe Experience Manager and SharePoint, standardized metadata tags enable search and filtering:

    FieldTypeRequiredPurposeExamples
    Document TypeDropdownYesClassify contentSOP, Training, Template, Brand Guideline
    Functional AreaDropdownYesGroup by workflowIntake, DAM, Legal, QA, Vendor Management
    RegionMulti-selectNoRegional applicabilityGlobal, Americas, EMEA, APAC
    Tool/PlatformMulti-selectNoAssociated toolsAEM, Workfront, InDesign, SharePoint
    AudienceDropdownYesAccess levelAll Team, Leads Only, Ops Only, Leadership
    StatusDropdownYesLifecycle stateActive, Under Review, Archived
    Last Review DateDateYesGovernance tracking[Date]
    Next Review DateDateYesGovernance tracking[Date]
    Metadata Benefits:
  • Enables faceted search (filter by region + tool + document type)
  • Supports automated review reminders
  • Powers usage analytics and audit reports

    • Access Control Organization

    Content organized by access level to support role-based permissions:

    Folder/ContentAccess LevelRationale
    SOPs & Process DocumentationAll Team (Read)Operational transparency; everyone needs process access
    Training & OnboardingAll Team (Read)Self-service learning; reduces bottlenecks
    Templates & FormsAll Team (Edit)Encourage template use; version control via naming
    Brand GuidelinesAll Team (Read)Brand compliance requires universal access
    Tool DocumentationAll Team (Read)Supports self-service troubleshooting
    Meeting Notes & DecisionsTeam Leads+ (Read)Strategic context; not all notes appropriate for entire team
    ArchiveOps Specialist (Full Control)Prevent accidental deletion; controlled access

    Scalability Considerations

    Adding New Regions

  • Nest regional variations within functional folders (not separate top-level category)
  • Example: SOPs > Legal & Compliance > LATAM-Regulatory-Compliance.docx

    • Adding New Tools

  • Create new subfolder under 05_Tool Documentation/
  • Mirror training structure under 02_Training & Onboarding > Tool Training/

    • Adding New Workflows

  • Assess fit within existing functional categories first
  • Create new Level 2 folder only if workflow doesn't align with existing structure

    • Team Growth

  • Taxonomy designed for function, not individuals or teams
  • Reorganizations don't require restructuring documentation

    • Implementation Outcomes

    Metrics (2018â€“2022)

  • Self-Service Success Rate: 95% of team located information without asking colleagues
  • Search Time Reduction: Average time to locate documentation reduced from 8 minutes to 2 minutes
  • Onboarding Efficiency: New team members navigated knowledge base independently by Week 2
  • Taxonomy Stability: No major restructuring required despite team growing from 20 to 40+ members

Supporting Global Operations

This taxonomy enabled asynchronous collaboration across time zones by ensuring critical operational knowledge was accessible 24/7 without relying on institutional knowledge held by individuals. The structure scaled seamlessly as the team expanded internationally, requiring only the addition of regional-specific content within existing categories.
