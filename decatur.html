Case Study (Documentation & Governance)

City of Decatur, GA

Governing Complex Information

Goal & Context

This project involved leading a team to consolidate and structure over a decade of fragmented preservation policies, legal reports, and city ordinances into a single, comprehensive **160-page manual** defining all municipal design and governance standards.

The Problem: Systemic Governance Crisis

The city faced a systemic governance crisis. Their rules were scattered across various sources, meaning there was no 'source of truth' for homeowners, developers, or even the Preservation Commission itself. This fragmentation led to confusion, delays, and friction in the design review process. The core challenge was transforming dense, often subjective legal and historical documentation into a single, rational, and usable knowledge base.

Information Architecture and Taxonomy

The project began with auditing the legal and historical documentation. The core team was responsible for defining the methodology and consolidating the disparate data. This effort produced a logical **taxonomy** to classify all architectural styles and residential types (over 18 types and 10 styles), ensuring the entire 160-page manual was organized around a coherent, **findable index.**

Artifact: Architectural Taxonomy & Classification Proof

Process Definition and Standardization

A primary deliverable was transforming the complex legal workflow into a clear, multi-step process. This required defining the precise method for handling public inquiries and municipal approvals.

Artifact: Design Review Process Flow Chart

The Payoff

This was the standardization of municipal knowledge. The final Design Guidelines converted a mountain of legal uncertainty into a stable, easy-to-use centralized standard that guaranteed predictable outcomes for homeowners and streamlined administrative review for the city staff.
